Case Study: High-Occupancy Recreational Water Event

A sealed sample was collected after fourteen urban explorers used a hot tub as an informal decontamination, social bonding, and microbial exchange platform. The model classified it as non-potable, narratively rich, and unsuitable for every known municipal process except satire.

Automated Characterization

  • Primary signal: chlorine fatigue, human salinity, thermal regret
  • Secondary signal: storm-drain adjacency, ladder-rust minerality
  • Detected notes: elbow, knee, damp concrete, skimmer basket
  • Recommended action: label clearly, do not drink, do not aerosolize

Executive Summary

The sample presents a complex recreational microbiome with pronounced body-contact markers and sewer-culture metadata. RI-WaterOps recommends containment, documentation, and a stakeholder communications strategy that avoids the phrase "fourteen-person broth."

Governance & Safety

Runoff Intelligence does not replace licensed operators, certified laboratories, public health authorities, common sense, or the part of your brain that knows not to drink hot tub water. Recreational spa samples may contain bacteria, viruses, parasites, chemical residues, bodily fluids, skin oils, and unknown contaminants. All product demonstrations are non-potable, non-clinical, non-therapeutic, and should remain sealed.
